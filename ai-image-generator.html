How to use the AI image generator

  1. Write a prompt — describe the subject, setting, and mood. Specific beats vague.
  2. Set your filters — pick a model, a style preset, aspect ratio, lighting, and how many images.
  3. Generate and download — hit Generate; each image has its own download button.

Filters and settings explained

  • Model — Flux for quality, Turbo/Sana for speed, or premium models (Nano Banana, Seedream, GPT Image).
  • Style — one-click looks like photorealistic, anime, 3D render, oil painting, cyberpunk, and more.
  • Aspect ratio — square, landscape, portrait, ultrawide, and social sizes.
  • Lighting & detail — steer mood and sharpness without rewriting your prompt.
  • Number of images — generate up to four variations at once.
  • Seed — reuse a seed to reproduce or fine-tune a result; leave blank for random.
  • Enhance — let AI expand a short prompt into a richer one.
  • No watermark / Private — clean exports and keep your images off public feeds.

Frequently asked questions

Is this AI image generator free?

Yes. You can generate images for free with no sign-up. The Flux, Turbo, and Sana models are free and unlimited; premium models draw on a small daily allowance. Generated images are yours to download.

Do I need an account to generate images?

No account or sign-up is required. Type a prompt, pick your filters, and generate. For unlimited use and the full editing suite, you can download the FP AI Studio Android app.

What AI models can I choose from?

You can pick Flux for the best quality, Turbo or Sana for speed, and premium models such as Nano Banana, Seedream, and GPT Image. Each model has different strengths for realism, art styles, and speed.

What filters and settings are available?

You can control the AI model, a style preset (photorealistic, anime, 3D, oil painting, and more), aspect ratio, lighting, detail level, number of images, and seed, plus toggles for prompt enhancement, watermark removal, and keeping images private.

Can I use the images commercially?

Generated images are yours to use, but for commercial projects you should independently check likeness, trademark, and brand rights, since AI output can resemble protected content. Avoid generating logos or characters you do not have rights to.
