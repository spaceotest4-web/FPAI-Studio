How to Create Consistent AI Characters (Same Face Across Images)

FP

10 min read

Consistent AI Characters

You've generated an amazing AI character — the face, the outfit, the vibe. Now you want to use that same character in a series of images: different poses, different backgrounds, telling a story. But every new generation gives you a completely different person.

This is the character consistency problem, and it's one of the biggest challenges in AI art. In this guide, you'll learn practical techniques to keep your characters looking the same across multiple generations using FP AI Studio.

Why AI Struggles with Consistency

AI image generators create each image independently. There's no "memory" of what your character looked like before. Every generation starts from scratch, interpreting your prompt from zero. Even identical prompts produce different faces, hairstyles, and proportions each time.

The good news: with the right techniques, you can dramatically improve consistency. Let's go through them from simplest to most advanced.

1. Write a Detailed Character Description

The foundation of consistency is a hyper-specific character prompt. Vague descriptions like "a woman with brown hair" will give you a different woman every time. Instead, define every distinguishing feature.

The Character Prompt Template

Build your character description using these categories:

  • Face shape: oval, round, angular, heart-shaped
  • Eyes: color, shape, size (e.g., "deep-set green almond eyes")
  • Hair: color, length, style, texture (e.g., "shoulder-length wavy auburn hair with side part")
  • Skin: tone, any distinguishing marks (e.g., "fair skin with light freckles across nose")
  • Build: height, body type (e.g., "athletic build, average height")
  • Clothing: specific outfit details (e.g., "dark blue leather jacket, white t-shirt")
  • Age: specific range (e.g., "early 20s")

Example: Building "Maya"

"A young woman in her early 20s named Maya, oval face, deep-set hazel eyes with long lashes, shoulder-length wavy auburn hair with a left side part, fair skin with light freckles across her nose and cheeks, slim athletic build, wearing a dark navy leather jacket over a white crew-neck t-shirt, silver pendant necklace"

Save this description somewhere — you'll paste it into every prompt. This is your character anchor.

2. Use Seed Control for Facial Consistency

FP AI Studio gives you control over the generation seed — a number that determines the random starting point for image creation. Same seed + same prompt = very similar output.

How to Use Seeds

  1. Generate your first image of the character
  2. If you like the face, note the seed number (shown in generation details)
  3. For subsequent images, enter the same seed
  4. Modify only the parts of the prompt you want to change (pose, background, action)

Important: Seeds work best when you keep most of the prompt identical. The more you change, the more the face will drift. Change one thing at a time — background, pose, or expression — not all at once.

Seed + Prompt Strategy

Structure your prompt in two parts:

Fixed part (character anchor): "A young woman in her early 20s, oval face, deep-set hazel eyes, shoulder-length wavy auburn hair with left side part, fair skin with freckles, navy leather jacket, white t-shirt"

Variable part (scene): "standing in a busy Tokyo street at night, neon lights reflecting, looking over her shoulder, cinematic lighting"

Keep the fixed part identical every time. Only modify the variable part.

3. Style Transfer for Maximum Consistency

This is the most powerful technique in FP AI Studio. Style Transfer lets you upload a reference image and generate new images that match its style, colors, and — crucially — facial features.

The Style Transfer Workflow

  1. Generate or select your best character image — the one that perfectly captures your character
  2. Use this as your reference image in Style Transfer
  3. Write a new prompt describing the scene/pose you want
  4. The AI will attempt to maintain the visual identity from your reference while applying the new scene

Style Transfer is especially effective for maintaining:

  • Facial structure and proportions
  • Hair color and style
  • Overall color palette and lighting mood
  • Artistic style consistency

4. Image-to-Image Generation

Another powerful approach: use your existing character image as a starting point for new generations. Image-to-image takes your input image and transforms it based on your new prompt, while preserving core visual elements.

Best Practices for Image-to-Image

  • Denoising strength matters: Lower values (0.3–0.5) keep more of the original image. Higher values (0.6–0.8) allow more creative freedom but may change the face.
  • Start low: Begin with 0.3 denoising and increase until you get the right balance between consistency and new content.
  • Crop to face: If you only need to keep the face consistent, crop your reference to just the face and upper body before using it as input.

5. The Multi-Shot Video Trick

Here's a creative workaround: FP AI Studio's multi-shot video feature (powered by Kling V3) maintains character consistency across video shots by design. You can use this to:

  1. Create a multi-shot video of your character in different scenes
  2. Extract individual frames as still images
  3. Use those frames as reference images for further image generation

Since multi-shot video is designed for storytelling, the AI works harder to keep the character looking the same across shots. It's consistency built in.

6. Combine Techniques for Best Results

The pros don't rely on a single technique. Here's the optimal workflow:

The Complete Character Consistency Workflow

  1. Define: Write your detailed character anchor prompt
  2. Generate: Create 10–20 variations until you get the perfect face
  3. Lock the seed: Note the seed of your best generation
  4. Expand: Generate new scenes using the same seed + character anchor + new scene descriptions
  5. Refine with Style Transfer: Use your best image as a reference for scenes where the seed alone isn't enough
  6. Fix with Image-to-Image: If a generation is 90% right but the face is slightly off, use img2img with low denoising to correct it

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Changing too much between prompts: Modify one variable at a time. If you change the pose, background, lighting, AND outfit all at once, the face will drift.
  • Forgetting specific details: If your character has freckles and you leave that out of one prompt, the AI won't add them. Be complete every time.
  • Using different models: Switching models between generations breaks consistency completely. Pick one model and stick with it for the entire series.
  • Ignoring aspect ratio: Different aspect ratios can subtly change facial proportions. Keep the same ratio across your series.
  • Not saving your best seeds: Always record the seed of generations you like. You can't recover them later.

Use Cases for Consistent Characters

Why go through all this effort? Consistent characters unlock:

  • Comic strips and webtoons: Tell stories with the same cast across panels
  • Children's book illustrations: Keep the protagonist recognizable across pages
  • Social media series: Build a recognizable AI character/mascot for your brand
  • Storyboarding: Pre-visualize scenes for film or animation with the same characters
  • Game concept art: Design a character and show them in various environments, outfits, and poses
  • Marketing campaigns: Use a consistent AI spokesperson across materials

Putting It All Together

Character consistency in AI isn't perfect yet — but it's far more achievable than most people think. The key is combining multiple techniques: detailed prompts, seed control, style transfer, and careful model selection.

Start with your character anchor prompt, generate until you find the perfect face, lock that seed, and expand from there. When seeds aren't enough, layer in style transfer and image-to-image. With practice, you can build entire visual stories with recognizable characters.

Open FP AI Studio, write your character description, and start generating. Your first consistent character series is just a few prompts away.
