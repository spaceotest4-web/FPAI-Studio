What Are AI Portraits?

FP AI Studio lets you transform ordinary selfies into professional AI portraits on Android — from business headshots to anime art styles — using text-to-image and image-to-image diffusion models, completely free. Upload any selfie, choose a style, and the AI generates a studio-quality portrait in under 30 seconds, no photography skills or expensive equipment required.

AI portraits use deep learning models (primarily Stable Diffusion and Flux architectures) to reinterpret your selfie in a target style while preserving your facial structure, features, and likeness. The image-to-image pipeline takes your photo as a conditioning input, then applies stylistic transformations guided by your text prompt — producing results that range from photorealistic corporate headshots to fully stylized anime characters.

How to Turn a Selfie into an AI Portrait

Converting a selfie into an AI portrait takes 8 simple steps in FP AI Studio. The entire process takes under 2 minutes from opening the app to saving your finished portrait.

  1. Take or Choose a Clear Selfie

    Select a well-lit selfie with your face clearly visible. Natural daylight or soft indoor lighting works best. Avoid heavy shadows, sunglasses, extreme angles, or busy backgrounds. A resolution of at least 512×512 pixels ensures the AI has enough detail to preserve your likeness.

  2. Open FP AI Studio

    Launch FP AI Studio on your Android device. From the main screen, navigate to the Image-to-Image feature — this is the core tool for selfie-to-portrait transformations.

  3. Upload Your Selfie as Reference

    Tap "Upload Reference Image" and select your selfie from your gallery. The AI uses this as the structural foundation for your portrait, preserving your facial geometry while applying the new style.

  4. Choose a Portrait Style

    Decide what style you want: professional headshot, anime, oil painting, watercolor, cyberpunk, vintage, pop art, or pencil sketch. Each style requires different prompt keywords and model settings (covered in the style showcase below).

  5. Write Your Portrait Prompt

    Enter a detailed text prompt describing the portrait. Be specific about style, lighting, background, and artistic details. Example: "Professional corporate headshot, studio lighting, neutral gray background, sharp focus, business attire, photorealistic, 8K detail."

  6. Select an AI Model

    Choose the model that matches your desired output. Flux 2 Pro excels at photorealistic headshots with accurate skin tones and natural lighting. Mystic produces superior artistic and stylized portraits. For anime styles, SDXL-based models deliver the best results.

  7. Adjust Settings and Generate

    Set image strength to 0.5–0.7 (lower = more likeness, higher = more style). Set CFG scale to 7–9 for good prompt adherence. Tap Generate and wait 15–30 seconds for your portrait.

  8. Refine and Save

    Review the result. Use AI Upscaler (2x or 4x) for higher resolution output. Use AI Relight to adjust lighting direction and intensity. If the result isn't right, tweak your prompt or image strength and regenerate. Save your favorite in PNG format for maximum quality.

Pro Tips for Better AI Portraits

These 10 expert tips will significantly improve your AI portrait results — based on how diffusion models process image-to-image transformations.

  1. Use even, soft lighting in your selfie — harsh shadows confuse the AI's face detection. Natural daylight from a window or overcast sky produces the cleanest results. According to a 2025 analysis by Stability AI, evenly-lit reference images improve output quality scores by 34%.
  2. Face the camera directly or at a slight angle (15–30°) — extreme side profiles lose too much facial geometry. The AI needs both eyes visible to accurately preserve your likeness.
  3. Set image strength between 0.5–0.65 for likeness — this is the single most important parameter. Below 0.4 produces near-copies of your selfie. Above 0.75 creates beautiful portraits that don't look like you. The sweet spot is 0.5–0.65.
  4. Start with Flux 2 Pro for realism, Mystic for art — each model has strengths. Don't use an artistic model for headshots or a photorealistic model for anime. Match the model to the style.
  5. Use negative prompts wisely — add "blurry, deformed, extra fingers, distorted face, low quality" to your negative prompt. This steers the diffusion process away from common artifacts.
  6. Generate 3–5 versions and pick the best — AI generation has natural variation. The same prompt produces different results each time. Generate multiple versions and choose the one with the best likeness and style balance.
  7. Upscale after generating — run your favorite result through FP AI Studio's AI Upscaler at 2x. This sharpens facial details, adds skin texture, and produces print-quality resolution.
  8. Use AI Relight to fix portrait lighting — even after generation, you can adjust the lighting direction. Add a dramatic side light for artistic portraits, or even front light for professional headshots.
  9. Remove background clutter from your selfie first — a clean, simple background in your source photo helps the AI focus on your face. Busy backgrounds leak into the portrait and reduce quality.
  10. Include specific artistic references in prompts — naming specific artists, art movements, or film stocks (e.g., "Rembrandt lighting," "Kodak Portra 400," "Studio Ghibli style") gives the model stronger style anchors than generic terms.
