Which post types can you make?

Each uses the same generate-and-export flow but serves a different goal — a single post for a quick update, a carousel for a step-by-step story, and a quote card for shareable, save-worthy text.

What size should your Instagram posts be?

The two sizes that matter in 2026 are 1080x1080 for square posts and 1080x1350 for portrait posts. Portrait fills more vertical space in the feed and tends to draw more attention, while square stays the safe choice for carousels and a tidy grid. An AI Instagram post generator can output both from one design without you redrawing the layout.

How do on-brand templates keep a feed cohesive?

On-brand templates store your colors, fonts, and logo so every post you generate inherits them automatically. That turns a scattered feed into a consistent visual identity people recognize at a glance, and it removes the per-post chore of re-entering hex codes and matching type. Set the brand once, and the generator applies it everywhere.

Consistency is what makes a profile read as professional. A cohesive grid signals that an account is active and trustworthy, and it makes your posts recognizable even before someone reads the handle. To keep templates working for you rather than against you:

  1. Lock a small palette — two or three brand colors, used the same way across posts
  2. Choose one or two fonts — a headline face and a body face, no more
  3. Place the logo consistently — same corner, same size, so it never competes with the message
  4. Reuse layout zones — keep headlines, images, and captions in predictable spots
  5. Vary content, not structure — change the message while the framework stays steady

How do you build a carousel that reads as one story?

A strong carousel uses one idea per slide, a shared template across all slides, and a cover that earns the first swipe. An AI Instagram post generator can produce a matching set of slides at once, so colors, fonts, and grid stay identical and the whole post feels like a single connected story rather than a pile of separate images.

  • Lead with the cover — the first slide decides whether anyone swipes, so put the hook here
  • One idea per slide — a single point, tip, or step keeps each frame readable
  • Keep text short — a headline and a line or two, sized to read on a phone
  • Hold the layout steady — same template across slides signals a deliberate sequence
  • End with a prompt — a final slide that asks for a save, follow, or comment

Carousels are one of the most save-friendly formats on the platform because people swipe back to multi-slide posts. Producing the full set from one template is faster than designing each slide alone, and it guarantees the visual continuity that makes a carousel feel intentional.

How do you design quote graphics that stop the scroll?

A quote graphic works when the text is the design — large, legible type on a clean or simple background, with enough contrast to read in a fast scroll. Keep the quote short, give it room to breathe, and let one or two brand colors do the styling. An AI Instagram post generator handles the spacing and sizing so the words land cleanly.

  • Keep it short — a quote that fits in one glance beats a paragraph nobody finishes
  • Maximize contrast — dark text on light, or light on dark, never low-contrast pairings
  • Give it space — generous margins make text feel premium and readable
  • Attribute clearly — a small, consistent credit line below the quote
  • Match your palette — quote cards are easy wins for brand consistency

Quote graphics are among the most reshared post types because they are easy to relate to and quick to consume. A clean, on-brand quote card costs little to produce and can travel far beyond your own followers when people share it to their stories.

What makes a post scroll-stopping?

A scroll-stopping post wins in the first half-second through visual contrast, a clear focal point, and a headline that promises something useful. People decide whether to stop before they read a caption, so the image has to carry the hook on its own. Design for the thumbnail, not the close-up.

  1. Lead with contrast — bold color or strong light-and-dark catches the eye mid-scroll
  2. One focal point — a single subject or headline, not a crowded composition
  3. Readable at thumbnail size — if the headline blurs when shrunk, it is too small
  4. Promise value in the first line — tell people what they get by stopping
  5. Favor portrait when it fits — 1080x1350 occupies more screen and pulls more attention

When does an AI post generator fall short?

An AI Instagram post generator struggles with highly bespoke art direction, dense data layouts, and copy it cannot write in your exact voice. The tool excels at fast, on-brand, repeatable posts; it is weaker when a design needs hand-crafted illustration, precise infographic alignment, or messaging that only you can phrase.

  • Bespoke art direction — fully custom illustration or unusual layouts still need a designer
  • Dense infographics — charts with many precise data points are hard to lay out automatically
  • Brand-voice copy — generate the layout, but write the words yourself
  • Untouched templates — publishing a template as-is is what makes posts look generic

The practical rule: let the generator do the layout and sizing, then add the human touches — your photo, your palette, your words — that make a post unmistakably yours. The speed comes from the AI; the originality comes from you.
