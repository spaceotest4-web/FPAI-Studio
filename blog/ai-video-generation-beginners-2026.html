The Prompt Writing Formula for Beginners

Good prompts are the secret to great AI videos. Use this simple formula:

[Subject] + [Action] + [Environment] + [Mood/Style]

Example: "A cat (subject) walking slowly (action) through a sunlit garden (environment), peaceful and dreamy atmosphere (mood)"

Beginner-Friendly Prompt Templates

Nature Scenes (Easiest)

  • "[Weather] in a [landscape], [time of day], [camera movement]"
  • Example: "Gentle rain in a forest, early morning, camera slowly moving forward"

Simple Objects

  • "A [object] [action] on a [surface], [lighting], close-up shot"
  • Example: "A red apple rolling on a wooden table, soft window light, close-up shot"

Character Actions

  • "A person [action] in [location], [clothing description], [camera angle]"
  • Example: "A person reading a book in a cozy library, wearing a sweater, medium shot"

Common Beginner Mistakes (and How to Fix Them)

Mistake Why It Happens How to Fix
Too complex prompts Trying to describe everything Start with 1-2 main elements
Unrealistic motion Asking for impossible physics Stick to natural movements
Multiple subjects Confusing the AI Focus on one main subject
Fast scene changes Cramming too much action One continuous scene per video
Vague descriptions Not enough detail Add specific colors, lighting

Settings Explained for Beginners

Duration

  • 2-3 seconds: Quick loops, simple motions
  • 4-5 seconds: Standard videos (recommended for beginners)
  • 6-10 seconds: Extended scenes (try after mastering shorter videos)

Aspect Ratio

  • 16:9 — Landscape (YouTube, TV, general use)
  • 9:16 — Portrait (TikTok, Instagram Reels, Stories)
  • 1:1 — Square (Instagram posts)

Motion Amount

  • Low: Subtle movements (breathing, swaying)
  • Medium: Natural motion (walking, flowing water) — best for beginners
  • High: Dynamic action (running, explosions)

Troubleshooting Common Issues

"My video looks weird/distorted"

Solution: Simplify your prompt. Remove conflicting descriptions and focus on one main action.

"The motion is too fast/slow"

Solution: Adjust motion amount setting or add speed descriptors to your prompt ("slowly", "quickly", "gradually").

"Characters look unrealistic"

Solution: Use Kling V3 Pro for human subjects. Keep character descriptions simple and avoid extreme poses.

"Wrong aspect ratio for my needs"

Solution: Always set aspect ratio before generating. FP AI Studio can't change it after generation.
