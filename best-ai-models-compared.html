Mystic: The All-Rounder

Mystic is Freepik's native model, purpose-built for generating commercial-quality images. It produces clean, well-composed, professional-looking results that work well for marketing materials, social media content, and business presentations.

Strengths: Consistent quality, clean compositions, great color palettes, reliable text rendering in images.

Weaknesses: Less creative flair than specialized models, can feel "safe" for artistic projects.

Best prompt style: Descriptive, straightforward. "Professional photo of a modern office with natural light, clean minimal aesthetic."

Flux Family: Precision & Photorealism

Flux 2 Pro

The quality leader. Flux 2 Pro produces the most detailed, photorealistic results of any model in the lineup. It handles complex prompts with multiple subjects, specific spatial relationships, and detailed environments better than any other model.

Best for: Final production images where quality matters most. Portfolio work, hero images, print materials.

Flux 2 Turbo

The balanced option. Nearly the quality of Flux Pro at significantly faster generation speeds. When you're iterating on prompts and need quick feedback, Turbo is ideal. Once you've nailed the prompt, switch to Pro for the final version.

Best for: Prompt iteration, social media content, when time matters.

Flux Dev

The experimental sibling. Flux Dev sometimes produces unexpected and interesting interpretations that the production models wouldn't. It's worth trying when you want to explore creative directions beyond conventional photorealism.

Best for: Creative exploration, finding unique angles on a concept.

Seedream V4.5: The Artist

If you want your images to have an artistic quality — painterly textures, dramatic lighting, illustrative flair — Seedream is the go-to model. It excels at fantasy art, conceptual illustrations, character designs, and anything that benefits from a non-photographic aesthetic.

Strengths: Beautiful artistic rendering, strong with fantasy/sci-fi, great lighting and atmosphere, unique visual character.

Weaknesses: Less photorealistic, can over-stylize simple requests.

Best prompt style: Artistic and evocative. "Ethereal forest spirit with glowing antlers, surrounded by fireflies, fantasy art, moonlit scene, detailed digital painting."

HyperFlux: Speed Champion

When you need results in seconds rather than minutes, HyperFlux delivers. The quality is lower than the other models, but it's more than sufficient for concept validation, mood boards, and rapid ideation.

Strengths: Extremely fast generation, good enough for brainstorming.

Weaknesses: Lower detail and fidelity, may miss nuances in complex prompts.

Best for: Rapid prototyping, testing prompt ideas before committing to a slower model.

Which Model Should You Choose?

Here's a decision framework:

  • Need the highest quality? → Flux 2 Pro
  • Want reliable commercial images? → Mystic
  • Creating artistic/fantasy content? → Seedream V4.5
  • Iterating on ideas quickly? → Flux 2 Turbo or HyperFlux
  • Exploring creative directions? → Flux Dev
  • Tight deadline, need speed? → HyperFlux

The best approach is often to use multiple models. Start with HyperFlux or Turbo to test your prompt, refine with Flux Dev or Seedream for creative options, then produce the final version with Flux 2 Pro or Mystic.
