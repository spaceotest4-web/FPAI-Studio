10 Prompt Engineering Tips for Better AI Images

Prompt Engineering Tips

The difference between a mediocre AI image and a stunning one often comes down to how you write your prompt. Here are 10 techniques that will dramatically improve your results in FP AI Studio.

1. Follow the Subject-Setting-Style Formula

Structure every prompt with three core elements:

  • Subject — What is the main focus?
  • Setting — Where is it? What's the environment?
  • Style — How should it look? What medium or aesthetic?
Example: "A samurai warrior [subject] standing on a cliff overlooking a misty valley [setting], ukiyo-e woodblock print style with vibrant colors [style]"

2. Specify Lighting Explicitly

Lighting transforms images more than almost any other element. Always mention it:

  • Golden hour — Warm, soft, flattering (great for portraits and landscapes)
  • Dramatic rim lighting — Creates depth and silhouettes
  • Studio lighting — Clean, professional, even illumination
  • Neon glow — Cyberpunk, futuristic, colorful
  • Moonlight — Moody, cool tones, mysterious

3. Use Camera & Lens Language

AI models understand photography terminology. Use it to control composition:

  • "shot on 85mm lens, shallow depth of field" — Blurred background portrait
  • "wide-angle lens, 24mm" — Expansive landscapes, architectural shots
  • "macro photography, extreme close-up" — Tiny details, insects, textures
  • "aerial drone shot, bird's eye view" — Top-down perspective
  • "low angle shot, looking up" — Makes subjects look powerful

4. Add Quality Boosters

Certain keywords consistently improve output quality:

  • "highly detailed", "intricate details"
  • "8K resolution", "ultra HD"
  • "award-winning photography"
  • "masterpiece", "best quality"
  • "sharp focus", "crisp details"

Don't overuse them — pick 2-3 that are most relevant to your image.

5. Describe Colors and Mood

Colors set the emotional tone of your image. Be explicit:

  • "warm earth tones, amber and sienna" — Cozy, rustic
  • "cool blue and silver palette" — Calm, corporate, tech
  • "vibrant neon colors, electric pink and cyan" — Energetic, nightlife
  • "muted pastels, soft pink and lavender" — Gentle, feminine
  • "monochromatic, black and white with one red accent" — Dramatic focus

6. Use Negative Prompts Wisely

When available, negative prompts tell the AI what to avoid. Common negative prompt elements:

"blurry, low quality, distorted, watermark, text overlay, extra fingers, deformed hands"

Keep negative prompts focused on real problems you're encountering rather than listing everything imaginable.

7. Reference Specific Art Styles

Name specific styles or movements for consistent results:

  • "Art Nouveau", "Bauhaus", "Art Deco"
  • "Studio Ghibli style", "Pixar 3D render"
  • "Impressionist painting", "Baroque oil painting"
  • "Cyberpunk aesthetic", "Steampunk machinery"
  • "Minimalist flat illustration", "Isometric design"

8. Control Composition with Position Words

Guide the AI on spatial arrangement:

  • "centered composition" — Subject in the middle
  • "rule of thirds" — Subject off-center for visual interest
  • "symmetrical layout" — Mirror-balanced design
  • "foreground, midground, background" — Specify depth layers
  • "negative space around the subject" — Minimalist framing

9. Iterate: Don't Expect Perfection on Try One

Professional AI artists iterate extensively. Here's a workflow:

  1. First pass: Short prompt, HyperFlux — test the basic concept
  2. Second pass: Add details, try Flux Turbo — refine direction
  3. Third pass: Full detailed prompt, Flux Pro or Seedream — final quality
  4. Polish: Use the upscaler and relight tools in FP AI Studio

10. Build a Prompt Library

Save prompts that work well. Over time, you'll develop a personal library of reliable formulas:

  • A "portrait formula" that consistently produces great faces
  • A "landscape formula" for stunning scenery
  • A "product shot formula" for commercial photography
  • Style modifiers that work well with specific models

The best AI artists aren't necessarily the most creative — they're the most systematic about what works.

Putting It All Together

Here's a prompt using all these techniques:

"A regal Persian cat sitting on a velvet throne in a grand library [subject+setting], oil painting style inspired by Dutch Golden Age masters [style], warm candlelight illumination with dramatic chiaroscuro [lighting], shot at eye level, centered composition [composition], rich burgundy and gold color palette [colors], highly detailed fur texture, masterpiece quality [quality boosters]"

Open FP AI Studio, paste this prompt, select Flux 2 Pro, and see the magic happen.
